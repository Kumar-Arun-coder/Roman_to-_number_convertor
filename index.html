About Roman Numerals

Roman numerals are a numeral system that originated in ancient Rome and remained the usual way of writing numbers throughout Europe well into the Late Middle Ages. Numbers are represented by combinations of letters from the Latin alphabet.

Basic Symbols

I = 1, V = 5, X = 10

Larger Values

L = 50, C = 100, D = 500, M = 1000

Example

MCMXCIX = 1999
